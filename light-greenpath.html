03 — GreenPath Financial Wellness · 2017–2021

$30M ARR. Team of 10.
Four years building a fintech.

GreenPath is a national nonprofit offering financial counseling to 60,000+ clients a year. As Design Director, I led the entire digital product from onboarding to financial coaching tools — and built the team that runs it.

A nonprofit product with enterprise complexity

GreenPath serves clients in serious financial distress — people managing debt, navigating bankruptcy, recovering from financial crisis. The product had to meet these users where they were emotionally, while also working within the operational constraints of a nonprofit and the compliance requirements of financial services.

When I joined as Design Director, GreenPath had a Wix website and a legacy counseling intake process that was entirely phone-based. Four years later, I left behind a digital platform with a full onboarding funnel, an online counseling product, a financial health dashboard, and a 10-person design team to run all of it.

"Designing for financial stress means every unclear label, every extra click, every confusing form costs real trust — and people in crisis have no trust to spare."

What I built

Financial wellness onboarding. A guided digital intake that replaced the phone-first model. The flow took users from "I need help" to "I have a counselor" in under 8 minutes — collecting financial data, matching to the right counselor type, and setting expectations for the session. Built on behavioral economics principles: reducing cognitive load, breaking complexity into stages, using progress mechanics to keep users moving forward.

Online counseling platform. A structured session format for certified credit counselors and their clients. Shared workspace, financial data visualization, goal-setting tools, and action plan generation. Designed for low-bandwidth environments and accessibility compliance across WCAG 2.1.

Financial health dashboard. A personalized view of a client's financial trajectory over time — debt reduction, savings growth, credit score improvement. Visualization-first design that let clients see the work they'd done and the distance they'd come.

Building the team

I inherited one junior designer. I left behind ten, including two senior designers, a UX researcher, and a content strategist. Building the team was as much a design challenge as the product itself — establishing hiring standards, creating a design review process, defining career tracks, and building the culture of quality that let the team operate at the level the product needed.

Behavioral economics in financial products

My PhD research in behavioral economics and cognitive psychology wasn't academic background — it was directly applied here. Choice architecture, commitment devices, loss aversion framing, social proof mechanisms — these weren't marketing tactics, they were design decisions grounded in the behavioral science literature that made the difference between a client completing their financial plan and dropping off at step 3.

GreenPath is where I learned that the hardest design problems aren't visual — they're behavioral. Getting someone to face their financial reality, make a commitment, and take the first hard step requires a design that earns trust faster than anxiety erodes it.
