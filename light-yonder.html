The $30M exit.
Designed from scratch.

The problem worth $30M

Enterprise brands were drowning in social data. They had access to everything — Twitter firehoses, Reddit threads, news mentions — but no way to extract the signal from the noise at speed. Analysts were spending 80% of their time on data collection and 20% on insight. It was backwards.

Yonder AI was built to invert that ratio. Using NLP and machine learning, the platform surfaced the narratives, actors, and coordinated behaviors that actually mattered — in near real-time. My job was to turn that technical capability into something a brand strategist could act on Monday morning.

"The hardest design problem wasn't making it beautiful. It was making a political scientist feel as comfortable in the tool as a data scientist."

Starting from nothing

I joined in April 2021 as the founding designer. There was no design system, no user research, no existing UI. The engineering team had built a powerful NLP pipeline that nobody outside the company had seen. My first two weeks were entirely in discovery — talking to the target buyers (brand intelligence teams at Fortune 500 companies) to understand their actual workflows, mental models, and vocabulary.

The key insight: these users didn't think in data structures. They thought in narratives. They asked questions like "why is this brand suddenly toxic?" not "show me sentiment trends for this keyword over 30 days." The product had to meet them there.

Narrative intelligence dashboard — surfacing coordinated social behaviors in real time

The design principles that drove every decision

We established three principles early and held to them through every sprint:

1. Answers before data. Every view should answer a question, not present a dataset. We ruthlessly cut charts and tables that described what happened in favor of interfaces that explained why and what next.

2. Confidence at a glance. The target user needed to walk into a boardroom and say "here's what's happening." That required the UI to communicate certainty levels, source quality, and recency without making the user think about it.

3. The footnote earns the headline. Every insight needed to be drill-able. A strategist had to be able to show their work — trace any claim back to raw sources — without that complexity surfacing unless asked for.

What I built

Over eight months I shipped the full product: narrative intelligence dashboard, entity tracking, coordinated behavior detection, campaign monitoring, and a reporting layer that let analysts export boardroom-ready summaries in one click.

I also built the design system from scratch — typography, color, spacing, component library, dark mode, accessibility guidelines — so that when Primer AI acquired the company, there was a real foundation to build on rather than a collection of ad-hoc screens.

Narrative detail view — showing engagement spike patterns, key actors, and coordinated behavior signals

The acquisition

Primer AI acquired Yonder in November 2021 as part of their expansion into brand and corporate intelligence. The acquisition validated the core thesis: that NLP-powered narrative intelligence had real enterprise value, and that the design work — making that intelligence accessible to non-technical users — was a key part of that value.

The product design I built continued in production at Primer AI after the acquisition. The design system and component library I'd created became the starting point for Primer's rebrand of the Yonder product line.
